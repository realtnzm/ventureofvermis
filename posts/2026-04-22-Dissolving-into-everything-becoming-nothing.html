I fear that if I try to be too many things I'll end up becoming nothing.

There's a kind of fear that doesn't freeze me in place but instead scatters me in a thousand directions all at once. The fear of wanting so much, of streaching myself so thin that in the end, there's nothing left, just fragments of almost and not-quites.

It's the quiet terror of becoming a jack-of-all-trades but a master of none, of loving so many paths but not fully walking down any of them.

What if, in trying to be everything I dissolve into nothing? What if my passion instead of defining me, disperses me like mist?

But maybe just maybe, the fear is a lie. Maybe the wanting itself is proof of something deeper: the hunger for life too vast to be contained under a single label?

Maybe identity isn't about choosing just one thing but about weaving together all the things that call to me. Maybe the beauty isn't about being one thing but about being you.

A constellation of ideas, dream, and contradictions that don't have to fit neatly into one category.

I am allowed to contain multitudes. To shift, to change, to explore, to become. There's no finsh to identity, ONLY MOVEMENT.
