Presence of joy isn't the absence of pain. It's the acceptance of it. I feel like a lot us look at things in black and white. We like to look at things as opposites when they are really not. Its all spectrums and balance. Without joy there's no pain and without pain there's no joy --Because you'd have nothing to compare the joy or the pain to. If you could make a conscious decision to prevent something bad you'd obviously choose to, even if the bad thing happening would prove to be inconvenient for you. This is what I mean by spectrum and balance.

We go through suffering because we will be able to experience joy and that joy will come around eventually.

I think a beautiful part of happiness is accepting that the thing that gives you the most happiness could give you the most pain, and accepting that fact nonetheless, because in the end that source of happiness won’t go away even after tremendous pain. You will always have that source.

But doesn't this insinuate that a universe full of all evil and another full of nothing (evil or good) would be equal because they both have no good or joy? I think that bad things only boost how people feel about it. Joy nor sadness is reliant on the other one to exist.
